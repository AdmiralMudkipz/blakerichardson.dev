⚡ Cheap Energy

Policy plan for faster, cheaper, local energy production.

⚡
Issue & Policy: Permitting Reform / Streamline Bureaucracy

Implement New Policy — SolarApp+ (NJ Smart Solar Permitting Law A5264)

Do solar permitting via SolarApp+ utilizing the NJ Smart Solar Permitting Law (A5264).

Effects: Effectively automates solar permitting, saving both the township and residents thousands a year while streamlining permitting and construction to get desperately needed solar savings rapidly during our ongoing energy crisis.

Issue & Policy: Energy Production / Cut Red-Tape

Repeal § 225-132D(1)

Allow properties to have solar installations in which the principle use is the production of electricity via solar.

Effects: Unbans solar power plants.

Issue & Policy: Energy Production / Cut Red-Tape

Repeal § 225-132D(3)

Allow a property to have both ground and rooftop solar.

Effects: This is a completely arbitrary rule with no benefit. If a property can use more solar than it has roof space they ought to be able to build that much solar generation by right.

Issue & Policy: Energy Production / Cut Red-Tape

Repeal § 225-132D(4)(f), § 110-1.1J

Remove mandatory township review for ROW facing roof solar.

Effects: Removes unnecessary red tape around installing roof solar. This law needlessly involves the township in something inconsequential and makes solar installation more complicated for homeowners for no tangible benefit.

Issue & Policy: Energy Production / Cut Red-Tape

Repeal § 225-132D(5)(b)

Allow ground solar on <1 acre properties.

Effects: The feasibility of a solar installation should be decided by the property owner. This opens up ground solar opportunities for residents with smaller plots.

Issue & Policy: Energy Production / Cut Red-Tape

Modify § 225-132D(6)(a)

Reduce solar panel setback rule to the same as sheds, 10 feet from the property boundary.

Effects: Enables solar installations in areas with less land to work with. No need for excessively large setbacks.

Issue & Policy: Energy Production / Legalize More Local Production

Legalize Local Energy Infrastructure

In addition to the ordinance reforms listed above, legalize the following:

  • Waive local township fees for energy installations provided the property owner has funding/financing.
  • Community solar zones — allows developers to build mid-sized solar arrays on top of commercial buildings, parking lot canopies, marginal/non-preserved land without a variance, so residents can subscribe to these local solar arrays and save 15–20% on their monthly energy bills without modifying their own properties at all.
  • Windmills so long as they fit the neighborhood aesthetic.
  • Legally permit homes to encroach a few inches into their setbacks if that space is being used to add thick exterior insulation (“outsulation”), a high-efficiency heat pump compressor, inverters, or batteries. Houses must still follow the aesthetic of the neighborhood and/or Main Street.

Effects: Lowers energy costs for residents and businesses. Lowers the barriers to building local energy infrastructure. Offers affordable options to those unable or unwilling to build solar upon their own residences.
